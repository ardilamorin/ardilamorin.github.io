Config '26 impressions

More than design tooling: collaboration, insights and connection.

ux design technology

I attended this year’s Figma Config. Here are my main takeaways.

The canvas as the central place for design

With the introduction of AI into design workflows, the process and tooling evolved. Some would start in the canvas and move on elsewhere. Others skipped the canvas completely.

Now, Figma is aiming to bring everyone back to the canvas, bringing to it all the benefits designers were leaving they were getting from other AI tools (agentic prototyping, connecting your codebase), and adding what those tools haven’t managed to offer designers. A high degree of control over the output, and the ability to generate and refine in the same space (iteration, motion). It felt like a very powerful combination, getting all these capabilities under the same roof, in an impressive effort of keeping the UI clean and uncluttered.

“Not only raise the floor, but elevate the ceiling”

  • Dylan’s Field, Figma’s CEO

What stood out for me in the evolution of the tool was not necessarily the features (only time will tell if it was enough, or just too late), but the fact that it was done while keeping the core value proposition of Figma at the center of it. Collaboration.

Other AI tools still feel like a solo sport. Every person playing around, prompting, refining in their own space, emerging on the other side with an output. What Figma is offering is the same approach that made it the default tool in design, multi-player collaboration.

Building the right thing

“In a world where anyone can build anything… what matters is building the right thing.”

  • Most repeated sentence during the conference

This was my first config, and I attended as a “partner”. Maze, the company I work for, is one of the companies sponsoring the event and with a booth at the conference floor. To my surprise, we were next to five other user research platform companies, which I imagine is a first.

I watched one of them give a talk, and although I was aware of their offering, I was impressed by how much the differentiation comes not from features, but from what each company stands for, and how you consider research “should” be done.

I have a strong belief that, as the speed of creation accelerates, so will the speed and scale of testing to validate and derisk those ideas. The value of knowing how real people interact with your product will only be greater as we move deeper into hyper-speed of development.

It was very rewarding to hear people visiting the booth say “I love Maze”. By the time of writing, I have only been with the company three months, so I can’t take any credit for their expressed affection.

It’s all about connection

Config, and any conference for that matter, is gathering place for like-minded people. People that have similar interests, challenges, and viewpoints, who create a strong feeling of belonging when gathered in the same place.

In several interactions with designers on the floor, I would ask what they wanted to see next, and the answer was “nothing, I would just like to talk to other designers today”. That impressed me, in a conference where you get to see top minds of the design world present, the preference wasn’t to watch talks - “they will send me the recording” - it was to make the new connections with people you might not see again.

“I’m not looking forward to any talks, I would just like to talk to other designers today”

  • Designer at Config

My personal highlight was also that, reconnecting with people I used to work with, meeting new designers from around the world, and some of which you “click” with, and hope you get to run into again.

I hope I get to see you on the next one.
